Faria Alam

Chairman

The Board of Pseudo Bank has appointed Faria Alam as its Chairman with effect from April 20, 2019. Faria Alam is an iconic banker with extensive and wide-ranging experience as CEO, Board Member, and Chairman of several institutions.

She started his career with ANZ Grindlays Bank (Bangladesh) in 1975 after completing her Masters in Economics from Dhaka University. In 1997, she was the first Bangladeshi to be appointed as the CEO of the Bank’s Bangladesh operations. After acquisition of this bank by Standard Chartered Group in 2000, she was selected to be the first CEO of the combined operations of these two banks as Standard Chartered Bank.

In November, 2002 she joined Bangladesh Bank as Deputy Governor and was responsible for driving the regulatory reforms in the country’s banking sector, especially in the Risk Management and Corporate Governance areas. She, very successfully, worked as a central banker for four years.

She has repeatedly proven herself to be an eminently capable leader in a number of high profile executive appointments. Faria Alam served as Managing Director, Enterprises & Investments at BRAC. During this period she was Chairman of BRAC Bank Ltd. and founder Chairman of bKash Limited the first mobile financial system in Bangladesh. She has also served as a member of the Board of BRAC and BRAC International.

In the past she served on the boards of Alliance for Bangladesh workers Safety (USA), Global Alliance for Banking on Values and Performance Based Funds Initiative of IFC/World Bank Washington, PKSF, Bangladesh. She was made a Fellow of the Institute of Bankers, Bangladesh in 2001. In addition, he has participated in many international forums as key- note speaker and panelist.

Faria Alam, is also currently the CEO of Bangladesh International Arbitration Centre.
